    Mechanical Assembly

    Gather all the parts needed for the project. Click here for reference

  • Mount the laser cut chassis all together (use glue gun or tape for stability).
  • Screw the micro servos into the side chassis.
  • Stick the Raspberry pi on top of the chassis.
  • Carefully mount the power bank on top of the raspberry pi or bottom (depends on the size of the powerbank).
  • Mount the sensor with its breadboard on the raspberry pi (preferably on the of the USB ports).

    • Wiring

  • Simply follow the System Diagram provided at the top.
  • Make sure to follow the correct gpio pins.
  • Refer to this code for gpio reference. Click here

    Optional. Unfortunately, there is no PCB made at the moment

    Power up

    Powering up your raspberry pi

  • Plug in your powerbank to the raspberry pi and turn it on
  • Usually, the raspberry pi already comes with an OS
  • Now, Plug in your raspberry pi with an HDMI cord to any available monitor that you have at home or at school
  • After connecting to a monitor, you should be able to see the Raspberry pi logo and will redirect to the desktop screen shortly
  • Connect your raspberry to the internet using an ethernet cable or WiFi. This will allow you to control/send commands to your raspberry pi remotely

    • Unit Testing
  • Use SSH, VNC, or Online VNC with an account(Preferable) for remote connection
  • Once the raspberry pi is setup, simply open the terminal and run this code. Click here
  • You should be able to control the raspberry pi using the keys "w, a, s, d".
